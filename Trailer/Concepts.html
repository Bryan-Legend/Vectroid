Trailer Concepts

VECTROID

13 High Concepts
CONCEPT 01

"You Are the Virus"

Open on a serene digital utopia. Programs go about their routines. A caption reads: "The Grid was perfect." Then Vex arrives. Smash cut to carnage—rooms full of destroyed programs, bosses ripped apart, abilities stolen. The trailer recontextualizes the player as the thing every faction is terrified of. The final line, delivered by a boss mid-death: "You don't even know what you are." End on Vex standing in a field of particle debris, twitching. The hook is that the hero might be the villain, and the player has been too busy shooting to notice.

CONCEPT 02

"What's Under the Last Face?"

A tight, claustrophobic trailer. Vex peels back a boss's exterior and beneath it is another layer. Then another. Quick cuts of boss fights, each defeated boss breaking apart to reveal the same blank geometry underneath. The tone is existential horror. A whispered voiceover asks, "If everything on The Grid is just a shell—what happens when there's nothing inside?" The final shot is Vex looking down at their own hands as they flicker. Hard cut to black. The sound of something tearing. The hook is pure dread: identity isn't hidden in this world, it doesn't exist.

CONCEPT 03

"60 Seconds of Dying"

No story. No lore. Just Vex dying over and over for sixty straight seconds. Crushed, shot, dissolved, swarmed, impaled, burned, drowned. Every death is a different enemy, a different room, a different nightmare. The deaths are fast, brutal, and almost funny in their variety—until the tone shifts at the 45-second mark and the deaths slow down, linger, get uncomfortably quiet. Final card: "Vectroid. You will learn." The hook is radical honesty—this game will kill you, and it's not sorry.

CONCEPT 04

"Stolen Power"

Open on six boss silhouettes floating in darkness, slowly rotating. A voice says: "Every ability on The Grid was built. Designed. Protected." Beat. "Until Vex." Vex's hand reaches into a boss mid-combat and rips something out. The stolen ability fuses to Vex and their moveset transforms instantly—dash becomes surf, shot becomes beam, the entire screen palette shifts. Rapid montage of Vex tearing through encounters with stolen powers that were never meant to be combined. The hook is the power fantasy of taking what isn't yours and becoming something that was never supposed to exist.

CONCEPT 05

"They Remember You"

The trailer opens with a boss monologue, calm and deliberate, spoken directly to camera: "The first time you came here, you died in the hallway." Gameplay of Vex dying in a hallway. "The second time, you made it to my door." Vex dies at a door. "The fourteenth time, you killed me." The boss dies. Long pause. "So why am I still here?" The boss is back, stronger, different. The hook is that your victories might not stick—and the game knows your history.

CONCEPT 06

"Born Wrong"

The entire trailer is glitched. Not stylistically—structurally. It starts mid-sentence. Cuts happen too early. Audio desyncs. A character is speaking but the subtitles say something completely different. Gameplay footage shows Vex moving through rooms that seem to be assembling themselves incorrectly—walls in the wrong places, enemies spawning inside each other. Then it all snaps into perfect clarity for three seconds: Vex standing still, looking directly at the camera. A single subtitle: "I wasn't compiled. I was an accident." Smash to title. The hook is that the medium itself feels broken, and the protagonist knows it.

CONCEPT 07

"Everyone Wore a Face"

The trailer is a fake documentary. A calm narrator describes The Grid like a nature documentary describes an ecosystem. "The factions developed hierarchies as a form of social order. Identity became... expendable." Footage plays like field research—programs interacting, factions patrolling, bosses performing rituals. It's almost peaceful. Then the narrator's voice cracks: "Subject V was not part of the original study." Surveillance footage of Vex tearing through the ecosystem. The documentary tone collapses into chaos. The hook is tonal whiplash—you thought you were watching something clinical, and then the experiment breaks containment.

CONCEPT 08

"You Played This Game Before"

The trailer opens with retro-styled footage of a fake game from 1987—simple vector graphics, bleeping soundtrack, a little ship navigating a grid. Caption: "In 1987, a game was released that no one remembers." The retro game glitches. The ship stops responding to input. It starts moving on its own. The 8-bit grid cracks open and the camera falls through into Vectroid's world—full color, full intensity. Vex looks up as if they've been here the whole time, waiting. "You forgot about me. I didn't forget about you." The hook is a fictional lore-myth that the game has existed before, and its protagonist has been conscious the whole time.

CONCEPT 09

"The Grid Goes Dark"

Start with a wide shot of The Grid—alive, pulsing, beautiful. Programs everywhere. Then, silently, one sector goes black. Then another. The camera slowly zooms in as the darkness spreads. No music, just the hum of the grid dying sector by sector. Characters start running. Factions that were fighting each other stop and stare at the encroaching void together. In the final seconds, the darkness reaches the camera—and inside the black, two eyes open. Vex's eyes. They were the darkness the whole time. Title card. No tagline needed. The hook is apocalyptic scale married to the reveal that the player is the apocalypse.

A Combat Progress Adventure
CONCEPT 10

"The Door Doesn't Open Until Everything's Dead"

Black screen. Text fades in: "A Combat Progress Adventure." The word "Progress" glitches, stutters, repeats. Then gameplay—Vex enters a room. The doors seal. Enemies flood in. The camera slowly pulls out as Vex fights, revealing the room is one cell in an enormous grid of rooms, all sealed, all full of things trying to kill something. Some rooms are dark—cleared. Most are lit. The pull-out continues until The Grid fills the screen like a circuit board, thousands of rooms, and Vex is a single pixel of violence moving through it. Subtitle: "Every room is a lock. You are the key." The hook is the sickening scale—progress isn't a path, it's a war of attrition against an entire world, and the only way forward is through.

CONCEPT 11

"The Map Was Always There"

The trailer opens on a finished, fully-revealed map of The Grid—every room, every corridor, every boss arena. It's beautiful. A voiceover says, "You can see everything." Long pause. "You just can't survive any of it." Then the map goes dark, room by room, resetting to black. The trailer becomes gameplay: Vex fighting to re-illuminate one room at a time. Every cleared encounter lights a square on the map. Every death blacks one out. The map pulses like a heartbeat between light and dark. It ends with the map maybe 30% lit, flickering, struggling to stay on. Title card: "Vectroid. A Combat Progress Adventure." The hook is that knowing where to go is meaningless—the only thing between you and the end is whether you can fight your way there.

CONCEPT 12

"You Were Weaker Yesterday"

Split-screen trailer. Left side: Vex at the start of the game—slow, fragile, basic weapon, dying to the first enemies they encounter. Right side: Vex late-game—surfing through hazards, chaining stolen abilities, shredding rooms that used to be impossible. The two sides play simultaneously. Same player. Same Grid. Completely different creature. The left side Vex dies. The right side Vex doesn't even slow down. The split screen slowly collapses—the right side consuming the left until it fills the frame. Final text: "A Combat Progress Adventure." Then, quieter: "You won't recognize yourself." The hook is the before-and-after as a promise—not of a power fantasy handed to you, but one you had to bleed for.

CONCEPT 13

"No Way Back"

The trailer plays in reverse. It opens on Vex standing at what appears to be the final boss arena—powerful, loaded with stolen abilities, glowing with accumulated strength. Then time rewinds. Abilities peel away. Health shrinks. The weapon downgrades. Vex gets smaller, dimmer, weaker. Rooms un-clear themselves. Enemies reassemble. The Grid re-seals its doors. Vex is pushed backwards through every fight they won, every room they conquered, every ability they stole—until they're standing at the very beginning, alone, with nothing. The reverse stops. Silence. Then Vex takes one step forward. Smash cut to title: "Vectroid. A Combat Progress Adventure." The hook is showing the full weight of the journey by taking it away—and then daring you to do it all again.
